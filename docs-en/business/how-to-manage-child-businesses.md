# How to Manage Child Businesses

**Screen**: Business
**Role**: Business Owner
**Difficulty**: Advanced

## Overview

The child business system allows parent companies to manage branches and associated businesses. A child business can use the parent business's TPay configuration and tax ID (NIP).

---

## Managing from the Parent Business

### Viewing Child Businesses

In the **Child Businesses** section on the Business screen, you'll see:
- **Pending**: new association requests
- **Confirmed**: active child businesses

Each child business displays: avatar, name, city, and email.

![Child businesses list: Screenshot #37](../../assets/images/business/child-businesses-list.png)

### Accepting a Child Business

1. Find the pending request
2. Configure permissions:

| Permission | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Use parent's TPay** | Child business uses the parent business's payment gateway |
| **Use parent's tax ID for invoices** | Invoices issued under the parent business's tax ID |

3. Tap **Accept** or **Reject**

### Changing Permissions

For confirmed child businesses, you can change permissions (TPay, tax ID) at any time and save the changes.

### Removing an Association

Tap **Remove** next to the child business and confirm. The child business will lose access to the parent business's TPay and tax ID.

---

## From the Child Business Perspective

If your business is a child business:
- On the Business screen, you'll see a **Parent Business Association Status** section
- The status can be: pending, confirmed, or rejected
- You can **resend the confirmation email** or **cancel the request**

> **Important**: When your business uses the parent business's TPay, the KSeF section is hidden.
